Constructed a panel dataset covering 30 Chinese provinces from 2009 to 2021, employing a Spatial Durbin Model (SDM) to investigate the spatial relationships among rural financial development, labor transfer, and the urban-rural income gap.

**Key Contributions:**
- Tested spatial autocorrelation of the urban-rural income gap using Moran's I and generated local Moran scatter plots
- Found a significant negative spatial spillover effect of labor transfer on the urban-rural income gap (total effect: -0.016, p < 0.01)
- Conducted robustness checks by replacing the dependent variable with the Theil index, further confirming the reliability of findings
